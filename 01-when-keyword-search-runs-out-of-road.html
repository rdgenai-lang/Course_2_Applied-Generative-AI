When keyword search
runs out of road

For most of computing history, finding information in a document meant matching the words you typed against the words on the page. That worked for an internet of articles. It works terribly for the documents professionals actually deal with. Press play below — we'll show you why, step by step.

The new idea: turn meaning into a position

What if every phrase got placed on a giant map of meaning — close to phrases that mean similar things, far from phrases that mean different ones? Watch what happens to our four phrases.

Phrases that mean similar things end up close together. Phrases about pizza are nowhere near phrases about contracts. Position = meaning.

Two ideas worth remembering

You won't need the math. You will benefit from the property.

Definition
Embedding

A way for an AI system to represent the meaning of a piece of text as a list of numbers. Pieces of text with similar meanings get similar lists; pieces with different meanings get different lists. You will never need to see the numbers themselves. You will benefit from the property: search by meaning, not by spelling.

Definition
Semantic Search

A search method that returns documents based on what they mean, not on which words they contain. The query "how do I cancel my subscription" returns the policy section titled "Termination of services" — because they mean the same thing.
