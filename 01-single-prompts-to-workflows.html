From single prompts to small workflows

A single message to an AI is rarely enough for real work. This week we turn one prompt into a small system — one input that produces two different answers, in two different tones, for two different audiences.

The problem with single prompts

Real work is never a single step

A common mistake: thinking of AI as a "magic box" you give one instruction to. In real organisations, even a simple task hides multiple decisions. Let's see the difference.

What people imagine

⚠ But… what actually happened? Was anyone told internally? Was the case logged? What action followed?

2
A real-world example

When a delivery is delayed…

Imagine a logistics incident: a truck breaks down, and a delivery will be late by 2 hours. Notice how this one event needs many things to happen — in the right order.

Logistics disruption scenario

From a single event, four things must happen — none of them is "draft a reply".

🚛 Event: Delivery #4821 to Mr Sharma will be late by 2 hours. Truck broken down on highway.
1
Understand

Read the event, extract who is affected, how big the delay is, and how urgent it looks.

2
Decide

Should we reroute another vehicle? Offer the customer a discount? Just delay? The system picks one.

3
Brief the operations team

Direct, action-oriented: "Dispatch backup van. ETA 4:15 PM. Notify driver."

4
Reassure the customer

Warm, empathetic: "Hi Mr Sharma, your order will arrive a little late — here's why, and what we're doing about it…"

What is a workflow?

A workflow is a chain of AI steps

Workflows are how single-shot prompts grow up into real systems. The output of one step quietly becomes the input of the next.

Definition

Workflow (in the AI sense)

A chain of two or more AI steps where the output of one step becomes the input of the next — possibly with branching or human checkpoints in the middle. Workflows are how single-shot prompts grow up into systems.

4
What you'll build this week

One input. Two outputs. Two audiences.

This is the system you'll build this week. A single piece of information comes in. An AI workflow splits it into two parallel replies — one for each audience, in the right tone.

📥 Single input
"Delivery #4821 delayed 2 hours — truck broken down"
AI Workflow
⚙️
Audience A · Internal Operations team
Tone: direct, action-oriented
Dispatch backup van V-07 to route 14. Reassign driver Vikas. New ETA 4:15 PM. Update tracker.
🙋
Audience B · External The customer
Tone: warm, empathetic
Hi Mr Sharma — sincere apologies, your order is running about 2 hours late. Our team is already on it; new ETA 4:15 PM. Thank you for your patience.
5
The hardest skill — tone separation

Same facts. Different registers.

Same event, same facts — but two completely different audiences with different needs. Your workflow has to switch tone without leaking the internal message into the external one.

🚨 Same event reaches both audiences at the same moment
👷
Operations team
Wants: clarity, speed, instructions
Backup van V-07 to route 14. Reassign driver. New ETA 16:15. Notify hub.
⚡ Direct 📋 Action-first 🚫 No fluff
🙂
The customer
Wants: empathy, clarity, reassurance
Hi Mr Sharma — apologies, your delivery is running ~2 hrs late. Our team is on it. New ETA 4:15 PM. Thank you for your patience 🙏
💛 Warm 🤝 Empathetic ✨ Reassuring
Definition

Tone separation

The discipline of producing audience-appropriate output even when both audiences are responding to the same event. Same factual situation, expressed in two registers.
