Equalised Odds

Demographic parity asks "are outcomes equal?" Equalised odds asks a subtler, sharper question: "are the mistakes equal?" Let's watch a disease-screening AI to see why mistake-equality often matters more.

Step 1/6
Wrong in two different ways
Every prediction an AI makes can be wrong in two directions. A "false positive" raises a false alarm — a healthy person told they're sick. A "false negative" misses what's there — a sick person told they're fine. These errors aren't the same, and their costs aren't the same.
Scene 1 of 6
Definition

Equalised Odds

A fairness criterion that asks: across different groups, are the system's false-positive and false-negative rates roughly equal? Where demographic parity asks "are outcomes equal?", equalised odds asks "are mistakes equal?" — a subtler and often more useful question.
