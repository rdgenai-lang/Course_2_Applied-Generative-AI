Definition
Classification

The act of placing a piece of text into one of several pre-defined buckets. "Is this email a complaint, a question, or feedback?" — that's classification. "Which of our four product lines does this query relate to?" — also classification. The skill isn't in being brilliant once. It's in being consistent ten thousand times.

Lab 3 · Teach the Machine

Build your own classifier with 5 examples

Here's the magic: you don't need to train a model for weeks. You give the AI a handful of examples — five to ten is usually enough — and it does the rest. Try it. Add examples, edit them, and watch how the classifier learns.

Definition
Few-shot classification

A pattern where you give an AI tool a handful of example input/output pairs — five to ten is usually enough — and then ask it to apply the same classification to new inputs. It works astonishingly well for tasks that would have required days of model training a decade ago. The trick is in the examples and in the way you ask for the output.

Lab 4 · The Most Important Lab

When the AI is not sure, who decides?

This is the guardrail that almost never appears in the demo and always appears in production. A confidence threshold says: "If you're less than X% sure, ask a human." Move the slider. Watch the tradeoff. There is no free lunch.

Definition
Confidence threshold

A rule that says "if the system is less than X percent sure of its answer, route it to a human." It is the most important guardrail in any automated decision system. Set it too low and errors slip through. Set it too high and humans drown in reviews. The art is in calibrating the line — and reviewing it every week.

Lab 6 · Did it land?

Three questions to own this idea

There are no marks here. These exist so you can check your own understanding before you walk into a meeting and propose a triage system.

Q1. Your colleague says, "Let's automate 100% of the customer query routing." What's the strongest response?
Q2. You built a few-shot classifier with 5 examples per category. Accuracy is 78%. Your boss asks: "How do we get to 90%?" What's the cheapest first thing to try?
Q3. The marketing team wants the classifier to also detect "VIP customer" so those get priority. You only have 2 examples of VIP messages. What should you tell them?
📦

Classification is the workhorse

Triage, routing, and sorting are not the glamorous side of AI — they are where AI earns its keep. Consistency at volume beats brilliance once.

🎓

Examples are the program

You don't code rules anymore. You curate examples. The quality of your 5-10 examples per category determines the quality of your system.

🚦

Always set a threshold

Never ship a classifier without a confidence threshold. The demo will work without one. Production never does. Humans handle the uncertain — and the system learns from them.
