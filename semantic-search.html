When Keyword Search Runs Out of Road
Week 2 · Legal Contract Search

A lawyer types "termination clause". The contract says "right to dissolve". Nothing matches.

Keyword search works fine for articles and product pages. It fails the documents most professionals actually deal with — contracts, policies, clinical guidelines, regulatory texts. The phrase you type rarely matches the phrase the document uses. Keyword search punishes you for not having memorised the document's vocabulary.

" Semantic search finds the passage that means the same thing as your question — even when the words are different. This is the most important shift in legal technology since print volumes moved to Westlaw and LexisNexis.

5Build It Yourself

Three no-code tools. Twenty minutes. Your own documents.

You do not need to set up a vector database or write Python. Each of these tools handles the four-step pipeline for you. Pick the one that fits your existing stack. The workflow is essentially the same: upload, ask, verify.

📓
Google · Free tier

NotebookLM

Excellent for individual research and document libraries up to 50 sources. Best for ad-hoc analysis of contracts, case files, or policy bundles.

  1. Open notebooklm.google.com and create a new notebook
  2. Upload your contracts as PDFs (up to 200,000 words each)
  3. Ask questions in plain English in the chat
  4. Verify citations — NotebookLM links every answer back to source
🟧
Anthropic · Project

Claude Projects

Strongest for nuanced legal language and follow-up questions across complex contractual obligations. The custom instructions field lets you tune the persona.

  1. Create a new Project in Claude with a clear name
  2. Attach contracts to the project knowledge base
  3. Set instructions: "Always cite the section number"
  4. Ask questions across the entire corpus in one conversation
🟦
Microsoft · Enterprise

Copilot on SharePoint

The right choice when contracts already live in SharePoint and the firm's IT/InfoSec policy requires data to stay within the Microsoft tenant.

  1. Ensure contracts are stored in a SharePoint folder
  2. Open Copilot in the SharePoint UI or in Teams
  3. Ground Copilot to that specific folder via "/file"
  4. Query in natural language — answers stay within your tenant
6The Two Words That Matter

Two definitions worth remembering exactly.

You will encounter these everywhere — in tool documentation, in vendor pitches, in your IT department's risk assessments. Knowing them precisely lets you cut through the noise.

Definition

Embedding

A way for an AI system to represent the meaning of a piece of text as a list of numbers. Pieces of text with similar meanings have similar lists; pieces with different meanings have different lists. You will never need to see the numbers themselves. You will benefit from the property: search by meaning, not by spelling.

Definition

Semantic Search

A search method that returns documents based on what they mean, not which words they contain. The query "how do I cancel my subscription" returns the policy section titled "Termination of services" — because they mean the same thing.

7Faculty Reflection

A note from the front of the room.

Faculty Note
For the lawyers among you, semantic search is the most important shift in legal technology since the move from print volumes to Westlaw and LexisNexis. For the rest of you, it is the technology that will let your organisation's knowledge actually be found by the people who need it, when they need it.
The same skill applies in both worlds: the ability to ask a precise question, recognise an imprecise answer, and verify before you act.
