Definition

Few-shot classification

Give the AI a handful of input/output examples — five to ten is usually enough — and ask it to apply the same labels to new inputs. Works astonishingly well for tasks that needed days of model training a decade ago.

Definition

Confidence threshold

A rule saying "if the system is less than X% sure, route it to a human." It's the single most important guardrail in any automated decision system — almost never in the demo, always in the production version.

6Honest Failure Modes

Where this approach quietly breaks.

Every triage system has failure modes. The shameful ones are the queries the AI gets wrong confidently. Click each card to see what really happened.

⚠ Ambiguous mention
"My loan EMI got auto-debited from my card. Should have been from the savings account."
AI guessedLoans & Mortgages (88%)
ActuallyAccount & KYC (auto-pay setup)
The word "loan" carries strong signal. But the actual complaint is about which account is being debited — that's an account-settings issue. Keyword-heavy classifiers consistently miss this kind of intent vs. mention distinction.

⚠ Multiple categories
"Lost my card and now there are 4 transactions I don't recognise. Please help!"
AI guessedCard Issues (76%)
ActuallyBoth — Fraud is the priority
Two real categories collide here. AI picks the dominant one by text proportion. But operationally, "unrecognised transactions" must go to fraud first — a card replacement can wait, a fraud freeze cannot. This is a routing-priority problem the model doesn't see.
Click to see why →
⚠ Hinglish / code-switch
"Mera card block ho gaya hai by mistake, please activate kar do urgently — flight tomorrow."
AI guessedFraud & Disputes (62%)
ActuallyCard Issues (re-activation)
"Block" is a high-signal word in fraud examples. Without local examples in Hinglish, the model misreads the customer's situation — they're not reporting fraud, they're requesting unblock. Few-shot examples must reflect the actual linguistic mix of your customers.
Click to see why →
⚠ Sarcasm / tone
"Great service guys, only took 3 weeks to credit a refund of ₹200. Really feeling valued as a customer."
AI guessedAccount & KYC (54%) — neutral tone
ActuallyComplaint requiring escalation
The customer is furious but using polite words. Models pick up surface sentiment, not irony. Triage systems that miss escalations cost the bank more than mis-routed routine queries — a frustrated long-tenure customer might churn.

7Bring It Home

The three things to carry forward.

01

Triage beats brilliance

For high-volume work, consistent and fast is more valuable than occasionally brilliant. AI classification earns its keep here — not in writing novels.

02

Five examples is the new ten thousand

Few-shot classification reduces what used to take days of model training to a single prompt. The quality of those five examples now matters more than the quantity.

03

The threshold is the system

Demo systems show classification accuracy. Production systems show the confidence threshold. Always know where uncertain cases go — and to whom.
