Disparate Impact

Demographic parity and equalised odds are statistical lenses. Disparate impact is the legal lens — the one a regulator will most likely use when they come knocking on your AI deployment.

Step 1/6
It starts with a rule that looks fair
A rule, written in plain language, that mentions no protected group at all. It applies to everyone the same way. From the outside, nothing seems wrong — the rule looks perfectly neutral.
Scene 1 of 6
Definition

Disparate Impact

A legal and regulatory concept used in many jurisdictions to identify when a system produces materially worse outcomes for a protected group, regardless of whether it was designed to. It is the framing a regulator will most likely use when challenging an AI deployment in hiring, lending, or insurance.
