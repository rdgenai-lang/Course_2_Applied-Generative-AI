Module 03 · Semantic Search

When keyword search
runs out of road

A lawyer searches for "termination clause" — but the contract calls it "right to dissolve." A doctor searches for "shortness of breath" — but the guideline says "dyspnoea." Keyword search punishes you for not having memorised the document's vocabulary. There's a better way, and you can build it in twenty minutes.

★ The core insight
Keyword search asks the user to guess the author's vocabulary. Semantic search asks the user to say what they mean. The second is the way humans actually think.

Definition
Embedding

A way for an AI system to represent the meaning of a piece of text as a list of numbers. Pieces of text with similar meanings have similar lists; pieces with different meanings have different lists. You will never need to see the numbers themselves. You will benefit from the property: search by meaning, not by spelling.

Lab 3 · The Aha Moment

Words live on a map of meaning

Modern AI doesn't think in words — it thinks in coordinates. Each word becomes a point in space. Words with similar meanings sit close together. Click any dot to see its neighbours. Type a word to find where it lands.

Definition
Semantic search

A search method that returns documents based on what they mean, not on which words they contain. The query "how do I cancel my subscription" returns the policy section titled "Termination of services" — because they mean the same thing. The system measures the distance between meanings, not the overlap of letters.

Lab 5 · From Concept to Tool

Build this for your own documents in 20 minutes

Three tools, three ways to set up semantic search over your own files — no code, no procurement, no IT ticket. Pick the one that fits where your documents already live.

N

NotebookLM

Best for · Mixed sources

  1. Open notebooklm.google.com and sign in with a Google account
  2. Click "Create new notebook" and upload up to 50 sources (PDFs, Google Docs, web URLs, YouTube)
  3. Wait ~60 seconds while it reads and indexes everything
  4. Ask any question in plain English — it answers with citations to the exact passage
Why pick it: Best for research-style work where you want quotes and citations. Free tier is generous.
C

Claude Project

Best for · Conversational depth

  1. Go to claude.ai and click "Projects" in the sidebar
  2. Create a new project, then upload your contracts/policies as Project knowledge
  3. Add custom instructions: "You are a contracts assistant. Always cite the clause you're pulling from."
  4. Ask questions in chat — Claude searches the project knowledge and answers in context
Why pick it: Best when you'll have a long conversation, refine answers, or want the assistant to remember context.
M

Microsoft Copilot

Best for · SharePoint shops

  1. If your organisation already has Copilot for Microsoft 365 licences, you're halfway done
  2. Drop your files into a dedicated SharePoint folder (or use an existing one)
  3. Open Copilot in Teams or the web. Use the "/" command to scope a question to that folder
  4. Ask plain-language questions — Copilot retrieves and summarises from your files with links
Why pick it: Best when documents already live in SharePoint and your security team wants enterprise tenancy.

🗺️

Meaning has coordinates

Words and phrases become points in a high-dimensional space. Similar meanings sit close together. That single idea is the engine behind every "AI search" you've ever used.

🗣️

Stop memorising vocabulary

The point of semantic search isn't that AI is smarter. It's that you no longer have to know how the document phrased it. Ask in your own words. Let the system do the translation.

🛠️

Twenty minutes, no code

NotebookLM, Claude Projects, or Microsoft Copilot — pick the one that fits where your files live. The hardest part of this work isn't technical. It's deciding which folder to point it at.
